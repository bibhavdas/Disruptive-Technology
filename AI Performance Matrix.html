AI Performance Matrix
(Confusion Matrix)

Class notes on understanding how to evaluate Machine Learning models using TP, TN, FP, and FN.

When an AI (classifier) makes predictions—like diagnosing whether a patient has a disease or filtering out spam emails—it isn't always right. To mathematically measure how good the AI actually is, we use a tool called a Performance Matrix (commonly known as a Confusion Matrix). It simply compares the AI's Predictions against the Reality (Actual).

The 4 Basic Terms

Before solving any numericals, you need to identify these four basic components from the given problem:

  • TP (True Positive): The AI predicted "YES", and reality was actually "YES". (Correct)
  • TN (True Negative): The AI predicted "NO", and reality was actually "NO". (Correct)
  • FP (False Positive): The AI predicted "YES", but reality was actually "NO". (Wrong)
  • FN (False Negative): The AI predicted "NO", but reality was actually "YES". (Wrong)
Pro-Tip for Exams: Always calculate your overall totals first!
• Total = TP + TN + FP + FN
• Actual Yes = TP + FN
• Actual No = TN + FP
