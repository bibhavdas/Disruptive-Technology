Generative AI

Generative AI
Type Artificial Intelligence Branch
Core Function Content Creation (Text, Image, Code)
Input Method Prompt (English Language)
Key Models LLMs, GANs
Popular Examples ChatGPT, Google Gemini

Generative AI is a highly advanced branch of Artificial Intelligence that focuses on creating entirely new content based on past data it has learned from. Unlike traditional analytical AI that only categorizes or sorts existing data, Generative AI uses a user-provided prompt (typically in the standard English language) to generate original texts, images, audio, video, or complex programming code from scratch.

Popular Examples

The consumer market has seen a massive surge in accessible generative tools over recent years. Two of the most notable examples include:

  • Chat GPT: Highly optimized for text-based tasks. It is best utilized for code generation, conversational text generation, and practical daily applications such as email writing and formatting.
  • Google Gemini: Distinguished heavily by its native "multi-model" (multimodal) understanding. This means the architecture was built from the ground up to simultaneously process, understand, and generate outputs using both Text and Images seamlessly.

Three Phases of Generative AI

The Generation Phase in action: The interface where users provide text prompts to initiate output generation.

Generative AI operates through a distinct, three-phase technical lifecycle to turn raw data into conversational output:

  1. Training Phase: In this foundational phase, the AI is fed massive datasets—encompassing millions of books, images, websites, and articles. During this time, it mathematically learns the deep patterns, context, grammar, and structures of these datasets.
  2. Model Type: This dictates the specific neural architecture of the AI based on its goal. Most text-based generative AI relies on Large Language Models (LLMs), while image-based models commonly use GANs.
    (Wiki Note: Classroom notes often mistakenly refer to this as a Generative Adverbial Network, but the correct computer science term is Generative Adversarial Network!)
  3. Generation Phase: This is the user-facing phase. When you provide a prompt, the model analyzes the context, understands your needs, and generates words, pixels, or sound patterns step-by-step based on mathematical probabilities.

Large Language Models (LLM)

An LLM is a specific, highly scaled type of generative AI model trained on a massive amount of text data. It uses deep learning techniques to understand the nuances of human language and generate coherent, contextually relevant responses.

Pipeline of an LLM

Building a functional LLM from scratch requires a strict, sequential 9-step engineering pipeline:

Data Collection → Data Cleaning → Tokenization → Embedding Layer → Transformer → Training → Tuning → Evaluation → Deployment

AI Agents vs. Agentic AI

As artificial intelligence evolves, it is moving beyond simply answering questions in a chatbox to actively taking actions in the real digital world.

  • AI Agents: An Artificial Intelligent system that observes its environment through any third-party digital media, sensors, or other inputs. It processes this information, makes decisions, and takes immediate actions to achieve a specific goal (e.g., an automated trading bot or a smart home thermostat).
  • Agentic AI: A much more advanced, futuristic concept. Agentic AI refers to a system that can independently plan, decide, and take multiple, sequential actions over a long period of time to achieve a complex goal with minimal human intervention. It behaves like a true, self-correcting autonomous entity rather than just a reactive script.

Trivia & Study Questions

Test your knowledge on Generative AI concepts with these in-depth study questions. Both quick review answers and detailed explanations are provided:

Q: What is the primary function of Generative AI, and how does it differ from traditional AI?

Short Answer: To create new content (texts, images, video, code) based on past data, triggered by a prompt.

Detailed Explanation: The primary function of Generative AI is to create entirely new, original content (such as texts, images, video, or code) based on patterns it learned from past data. It differs from traditional (or discriminative) AI, which is typically only used to analyze, categorize, or sort existing data. Generative AI is triggered into action by a user-provided prompt, usually written in standard English.

Q: What makes Google Gemini distinctively different from standard text-based models?

Short Answer: It features "multi-model" understanding, allowing it to process both text and images simultaneously.

Detailed Explanation: Google Gemini is distinguished by its native "multimodal" understanding. Instead of relying on separate models stitched together to handle different types of media, Gemini was built from the ground up to seamlessly process, understand, and generate both text and images simultaneously, allowing for much more complex reasoning.

Q: What exactly happens during the "Generation Phase"?

Short Answer: After receiving a prompt, the model predicts and generates words, pixels, or sound patterns step-by-step.

Detailed Explanation: During the Generation Phase, the AI receives the user's prompt and begins an auto-regressive process. It analyzes the context of your request against its vast training data, and mathematically predicts the next most logical word, pixel, or sound pattern, generating the final output step-by-step.

Q: What is the defining difference between a standard AI Agent and Agentic AI?

Short Answer: Agentic AI can independently plan, decide, and take multiple actions over time with minimal human intervention, acting fully autonomously.

Detailed Explanation: The defining difference is autonomy and long-term planning. While a standard AI Agent observes its environment to make a single decision or reaction, Agentic AI is capable of breaking down a massive, complex goal into smaller steps. It can independently plan, execute multiple actions over an extended period of time, evaluate its own success, and adjust its strategy with virtually no human intervention.
